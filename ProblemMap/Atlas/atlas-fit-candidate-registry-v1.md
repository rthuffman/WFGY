# Atlas Fit Candidate Registry 🎯

## Problem Map 3.0 Troubleshooting Atlas
> Quick links, fit-status language, and beginner-friendly rules for describing the current best structural fit

This page exists because classification is not only about naming families.

It is also about describing **how strongly** the current case fits the available structure.

That sounds simple, but it is one of the places where systems start bluffing.

A weak system often does one of these things:

- it jumps from family hint to fake certainty
- it uses strong words to hide thin evidence
- it treats a helpful guess as if it were a stable fit
- it confuses “best current read” with “final closure”

This page prevents that drift.

It gives the Atlas a public language for fit status.

That language helps readers say:

- this is the current primary fit
- this is only a strong candidate
- this is still boundary-live
- this is only safe at family level
- this does not yet deserve promotion
- this case is still under-evidenced

That is the real job of a fit registry.

## Why this page exists

The Atlas already has:

- a family layer
- a boundary layer
- a subtree-control layer

That is strong, but one major practical question still remains:

once you have looked at those layers, **how should you describe the current best fit**?

That question matters because real cases are uneven.

Some are clean.  
Some are mixed.  
Some are thinly evidenced.  
Some sit on a boundary.  
Some clearly belong to a family but not yet to a safely promoted finer branch.

Without fit language, people often overstate what they know.

So this page exists to make fit description more honest, more readable, and more reusable.

## What a fit candidate is

A fit candidate is a current structural placement claim.

It says:

given the evidence we have right now,  
the case most plausibly fits here,  
to this degree,  
at this level,  
with these remaining limits.

That means a fit candidate is not just a label.  
It is a claim with built-in discipline.

A good fit candidate should tell the reader:

- where the case currently belongs most plausibly
- how strong that placement is
- what is still uncertain
- what level the fit is safe at
- what should not yet be overclaimed

So the fit layer is not there to make the Atlas sound stronger.  
It is there to make the Atlas more honest.

---

## Why fit language matters

Fit language matters because the Atlas is a route-first system, not a bluff machine.

In a route-first system, it is normal that:

- some cases are clean
- some cases are strong but not closed
- some cases are only safe at family level
- some cases remain boundary-live
- some cases should stay under-evidenced

If the system cannot say those differences clearly, then everything collapses into one of two bad extremes:

- fake certainty
- useless vagueness

This page exists to avoid both.

## Fit-status classes

This first public version uses a simple fit-status language.

---

## 1. Primary fit

### Meaning
This is the strongest current structural placement supported by the available evidence.

### Use this when
- one family or one public local branch clearly leads
- neighboring alternatives are weaker
- the available evidence is strong enough for a practical current decision
- the claim does not exceed the level actually supported

### What this does not mean
It does **not** automatically mean:
- final truth
- frozen-core promotion
- no remaining uncertainty
- no possible overlay later

### Good usage style
> current primary fit: F4  
> current primary fit: F1 family-level  
> current primary fit: F2-side boundary read

---

## 2. Strong candidate

### Meaning
A structural placement is notably plausible, but not yet strong enough to be described as the primary fit with high stability.

### Use this when
- the case leans one way, but not decisively enough
- a neighboring family or branch still remains live
- the evidence supports direction but not closure

### What this does not mean
It does **not** mean:
- weak random guess
- rhetorical hedge with no content
- hidden certainty pretending to be modest

### Good usage style
> strongest current candidate: F3  
> strong candidate on the F1 side, but not yet clean enough for full promotion

---

## 3. Boundary-live

### Meaning
Two neighboring structural readings remain live enough that a clean hard split would overstate the evidence.

### Use this when
- the case genuinely sits on a boundary
- the decisive separation question has not been answered well enough
- both sides still carry meaningful plausibility

### What this does not mean
It does **not** mean:
- confusion forever
- refusal to think
- lack of any route-first value

Boundary-live is still useful.  
It tells the reader where the real tension is.

### Good usage style
> current read remains boundary-live between F1 and F7  
> boundary-live between F3 and F4, pending stronger state trace

---

## 4. Family-level only

### Meaning
The safest current claim stops at family level.

### Use this when
- subtree or local branch detail would overstate the evidence
- the family is clear enough, but finer placement is not
- the public subtree layer is not yet stable enough here

### What this does not mean
It does **not** mean:
- the case is uninteresting
- no further work is possible
- the Atlas has no finer internal view

It simply means the **publicly justified claim** is still family-level.

### Good usage style
> current best fit is F5 at family level only  
> F6 family-level only for now

---

## 5. Referenced local fit, not yet promoted

### Meaning
A finer local reading may be structurally meaningful, but it is not yet strong enough to be treated as a stable public fit.

### Use this when
- a likely local branch is visible
- the pattern is real enough to mention cautiously
- public promotion would still be premature

### What this does not mean
It does **not** mean:
- the branch is fake
- the branch will never become public
- the reader should act as if the fit is already stable

### Good usage style
> likely local fit referenced, but not yet promoted  
> local branch signal exists, but current public fit should remain at family level

---

## 6. Insufficient evidence

### Meaning
The current evidence is too thin, blocked, contradictory, or poorly instrumented for a useful fit claim beyond a very limited level.

### Use this when
- observability is too weak
- the evidence surface is contradictory
- the input is too partial
- any stronger fit wording would be fake precision

### What this does not mean
It does **not** mean:
- the Atlas failed
- classification is impossible forever
- no next step exists

It means the right next step is often to improve the evidence surface first.

### Good usage style
> fit remains under-evidenced  
> insufficient evidence for stable fit beyond tentative F5/F6 boundary read

---

## Fit-status summary table 📚

| Fit status | What it means | Best use case | Main danger if misused |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary fit | strongest current structural placement | evidence clearly favors one route | pretending this means total closure |
| Strong candidate | one route leans stronger, but not decisively | direction is meaningful, closure is premature | hiding uncertainty with inflated wording |
| Boundary-live | two neighboring routes remain live | the real issue is still unresolved separation | forcing a fake hard split |
| Family-level only | safest current claim stops at family layer | family is clear, finer placement is not | over-promoting into fake local detail |
| Referenced local fit, not yet promoted | a finer branch may be visible, but not yet stably public | cautious local mention without full promotion | treating a tentative branch as if already stabilized |
| Insufficient evidence | current evidence cannot support stronger fit language | traces are weak, partial, or contradictory | bluffing certainty instead of improving observability |

## What fit status is not

This section matters a lot.

### Fit status is not the same as family naming
A family label tells you the broad structural region.  
Fit status tells you how strongly the current case belongs there.

### Fit status is not the same as subtree status
A subtree can be publicly available in principle, while the current case still does not deserve subtree-level fit.

### Fit status is not the same as confidence style
A confident tone is not a fit class.

### Fit status is not the same as repair direction
A useful first repair move does not automatically prove a strong fit.

### Fit status is not the same as promotion status
A case can fit a structure well without that structure already being promoted as a frozen public node.

---

## Evidence expectations for stronger fit language

Stronger fit wording should generally require stronger support.

### To move from family hint to primary fit
You usually want:
- a clearer dominant failure surface
- weaker neighboring alternatives
- enough evidence that the current route is meaningfully actionable

### To move from family-level only to a finer local fit
You usually want:
- a public subtree surface or clearly referenced local branch
- enough separation value to justify the finer statement
- enough evidence that the local branch is not just convenient wording

### To avoid inflation
You should pause when:
- observability is weak
- the boundary is still live
- the case mixes several strong surfaces
- the finer branch is not publicly stable enough yet

---

## Fit restraint rules

A fit registry is only useful if it includes restraint.

### Rule 1. Do not promote past the evidence
If the evidence supports family-level only, then family-level only is the right answer.

### Rule 2. Do not use stronger wording to hide thinner proof
A polished sentence is not a promoted fit.

### Rule 3. Do not confuse local intuition with public stability
A good internal hunch is not yet a stable public fit.

### Rule 4. Do not erase live alternatives too early
If a neighboring route is still alive, keep it visible.

### Rule 5. Better unresolved than falsely precise
A clean limited answer is better than a fake sharp answer.

---

## Common fit mistakes

### Mistake 1. Treating every plausible branch as a primary fit
Plausible is not the same as strongest current placement.

### Mistake 2. Treating every helpful clue as if it deserved local promotion
Local hints are useful, but not every hint deserves public fit language.

### Mistake 3. Using family certainty to fake subtree certainty
Knowing the family does not automatically justify a finer branch.

### Mistake 4. Confusing repair urgency with fit quality
An urgent need to act does not magically improve the fit evidence.

### Mistake 5. Letting style replace structure
A strong writing style can make a weak fit sound stronger than it is.

## Example fit wording patterns

These are not full output contracts.  
They are just example language patterns.

### Example 1. Clean family-level primary fit
> current primary fit: F4 at family level  
> neighboring alternatives are weaker, but finer local promotion is not yet needed

### Example 2. Strong candidate without hard closure
> strongest current candidate: F1  
> F7 remains a live neighboring alternative because the final representation surface is also degraded

### Example 3. Boundary-live
> current read remains boundary-live between F3 and F4  
> continuity loss and contract-execution failure are both active enough that a forced split would overstate the evidence

### Example 4. Referenced local fit, not yet promoted
> likely local branch signal exists under F2, but current public fit should remain at family level until separation is stronger

### Example 5. Insufficient evidence
> insufficient evidence for stable fit beyond tentative F5/F6 boundary read  
> observability weakness blocks stronger placement
